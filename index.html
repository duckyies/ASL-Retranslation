Smart Home Automation

Smart home automation systems are becoming increasingly popular, offering users enhanced control over their home environment through interconnected devices. This paper presents a novel approach to smart home automation using linked lists, a fundamental data structure, to efficiently manage and control various home appliances. In this system, each home appliance is represented as a node in a linked list, allowing for dynamic management of devices.

The linked list structure enables easy addition, removal, and modification of appliances, as well as real-time adjustments to their states, such as turning on/off, temperature control, or lighting adjustments. By leveraging the properties of linked lists, the system can scale with increasing numbers of devices while maintaining efficiency. The proposed solution is designed for ease of implementation and use, with a focus on minimizing resource consumption and enhancing system responsiveness. Experimental results show linked list-based approach provides an effective solution for smart home automation, offering improved device management and seamless user control.

ASL Translator

The American Sign Language (ASL) is a crucial mode of communication for the deaf and hard-of-hearing community. However, communication barriers often arise when non-signers interact with ASL users. This paper proposes an innovative approach to translating ASL into text or speech using a hybrid model that combines Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs).

The CNN is employed to extract spatial features from ASL hand gesture images, while the RNN captures the sequential dependencies in the gesture movements, improving the translation accuracy. The model is trained on a large dataset of ASL signs and utilizes a combination of image pre-processing techniques and advanced neural architectures to enhance real-time translation performance. Experimental results demonstrate the efficacy of this model in achieving high accuracy for recognizing individual signs as well as continuous signing, offering a promising solution to bridging the communication gap between ASL users and non-signers.
